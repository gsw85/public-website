BIOGRAPHY

Dr. Shu Wei Goh, the Founder of Universal Pave Sdn Bhd, was born on January 6, 1985 in Kangar, Perlis, Malaysia. Dr. Goh is a licensed civil engineer and entrepreneur whose business focuses on providing client-centered services specializing in asphalt applications. Universal Pave uses the most current research and information to apply cutting-edge technologies and innovative approaches for procuring asphalt materials and providing reliable consulting services to their clients. At present, Dr. Goh is a co-adviser to doctorate students who are involved in Warm Mix Technology research at the Universiti Teknologi MARA and the Universiti Sains Malaysia. He is a professional civil engineer, speaker, consultant and entrepreneur.

EARLY WORK EXPOSURE

Dr. Goh’s entrepreneurial side was nurtured by his parents, who instilled in the young boy the importance of hard work and determination. Dr. Goh’s parents owned a small coffee shop business in his hometown of Kangar, Perlis, Malaysia. From the time he was 5 years old until he was 12, the boy helped out in his parents’ shop, assisting in the daily operations as a helper. Wanting to earn more income as spending money, he also worked as a trainee at a computer shop in the same area, learning how computers worked along the way. As he progressed, he was later promoted to technician, inspecting and repairing computers for customers. It is in this computer shop where Dr. Goh learned coding as well as other computer applications, including computer graphics. He would later use his experience in this job to earn money as a graduate student and later co-found an IT company.

SPORTS AND OTHER SKILLS

Dr. Goh was also very athletic as a young high school student, winning top prizes in badminton and softball. From 1998 to 2002, he was part of the team that won the Perlis State Championship in Softball. In 2002, they also won in the National Open Competition for Softball in Malaysia. Not content with just building his athletic skills, Dr. Goh also focused on other activities that allowed him to use his interests. In 1993, he won third place in a mental arithmetic competition. In 1999, 2001, 2002 and 2004, he also placed first in a watercolor painting contest in Perlis.

Dr. Goh’s intellectual capacity was also recognized by MENSA. In 2004, he qualified for membership in the prestigious organization after passing a standardized test. MENSA is the world’s oldest and largest society that consists only of high-IQ members. The non-profit organization is based in Caythorpe, Lincolnshire, England, although it also has local national groups in a number of countries. Members qualify once they have undergone an approved supervised and/or standardized intelligence test and scored a minimum rating at the 98th percentile. MENSA members have included scientists, mathematicians, world-renowned researchers, businessmen and businesswomen, professors and even entertainers.

EARLY RESEARCH WORK

During his senior year as a civil engineering student, Dr. Goh submitted a proposal for a study of the sustainable development of transportation materials. The project proposed the integration of Warm Mix technology into the HMA or Hot Mix Asphalt process. After being reviewed, his proposal was accepted by the Civil Engineering Department of the Michigan Tech. University. Dr. Goh also published five papers during his last semesters. His papers were published by the International Journal of Pavement Research and Technology, the Journal of Transportation Material and the Journal of Geotechnical Engineering.
